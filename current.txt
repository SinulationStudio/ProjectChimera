   * Visuals: A row of five semi-transparent, rotating glyphs hovers at the bottom of the screen. A Verbal action might be a pulsing

   * How it Works: The glyphs provide at-a-glance information through their shape and color. When you focus on a glyph (hover over it), it

       * Less Room for Art: De-emphasizes the large, cinematic card art that is a staple of the genre, which could detract from the game's
         explicit nature.

  ---

  Idea 2: Minimalist Data Fragments

   * Concept: This is an evolution of the traditional card. The actions are still presented in rectangular containers, but they are
     re-imagined as floating, semi-transparent panes of holographic glass.
   * Visuals: The "cards" have no borders. The background is a subtle, looping animation of data streams or a faint schematic. The key
     innovation is the "art." Instead of a static image, the art space could be a live, looping video preview of the action generated by our
     GenAI pipeline, or a tactical wireframe view of the target area.
   * How it Works: The layout is familiar (cost top-left, name top, effect text bottom), making it instantly intuitive. The focus is on the
     futuristic material of the card itself and the dynamic content in the art space.
   * Pros:
       * The Best of Both Worlds: Intuitive for anyone who has played a card game, but with a strong, unique thematic twist.
       * Perfect for GenAI: Directly supports the LDD's plan to use GenAI for generating visual card content.
       * Balanced: Provides ample space for clear text and impactful, explicit visuals.
   * Cons:
       * Less Innovative: Could be seen as a "safer," less radical departure from the norm.

  Idea 3: Command-Line Actions

   * Concept: Leans hard into the "computer" aspect. The player's hand is a list of executable commands in a sleek, stylized terminal

   * How it Works: You select a command with the mouse or arrow keys. Highlighting a command could bring up a detailed tooltip with the full
     description and a small visualizer. Keywords and effects could be color-coded within the text itself for readability.
   * Pros:
